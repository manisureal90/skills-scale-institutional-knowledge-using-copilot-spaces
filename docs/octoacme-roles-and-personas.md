# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Key Interactions
- Works with UX/UI Designers to implement design specs and ensure usability
- Collaborates with QA/Testing to ensure acceptance criteria are met
- Coordinates with DevOps Engineers on deployment and infrastructure requirements
- Receives requirements from Business Analysts

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Key Interactions
- Works with UX/UI Designers during backlog grooming and user research
- Receives business requirements from Business Analysts
- Collaborates with Support/Customer Success on user feedback and priorities
- Partners with Project Managers on timelines and resource allocation

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Key Interactions
- Partners with Product Managers on planning and prioritization
- Escalates blockers and dependencies identified by DevOps, QA, and Developer teams
- Coordinates incident response with Support/Customer Success
- Ensures cross-functional alignment during retrospectives

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between stakeholder needs and technical implementation. They translate business requirements into clear, actionable specifications and ensure that solutions align with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and gather business requirements
- Translate business needs into technical requirements and user stories
- Define acceptance criteria in collaboration with Developers and Product Managers
- Identify and document dependencies and constraints
- Act as a liaison between non-technical stakeholders and the delivery team
- Validate solutions against original business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure solution clarity and alignment with business goals
- Reduce ambiguity and rework due to unclear requirements
- Facilitate faster decision-making by clarifying trade-offs
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Requirement specification documents
- User story refinement sessions
- Requirements traceability discussions

### Key Interactions
- Works with Product Managers to clarify scope and prioritization
- Partners with Developers and UX/UI Designers during requirements definition
- Supports Project Managers in identifying dependencies and risks
- Collaborates with Support/Customer Success to incorporate user insights

---

## UX/UI Designers

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers are responsible for understanding user needs, creating intuitive designs, and ensuring that products are both functional and delightful. They conduct research, create prototypes, and collaborate closely with product and engineering teams.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and design specifications
- Define interaction patterns and user flows
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design principles
- Collaborate with Developers to ensure design feasibility
- Iterate on designs based on feedback and metrics

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered designs
- Reduce user friction and support tickets through usability
- Ensure accessibility compliance and inclusive experiences
- Maintain design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design specs and component libraries
- User research findings and personas
- Collaboration sessions during sprint planning
- Design review and feedback cycles
- Accessibility requirements documentation

### Key Interactions
- Works closely with Product Managers during backlog grooming to understand user needs
- Collaborates with Developers during implementation to ensure design fidelity
- Partners with QA/Testing on usability acceptance criteria
- Receives feedback from Support/Customer Success on user pain points

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and deployment automation that enable reliable, scalable software delivery. They ensure that code moves smoothly from development to production with minimal manual intervention.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage cloud infrastructure and configuration as code
- Automate deployment, monitoring, and rollback processes
- Ensure system reliability, security, and performance
- Provide support for production incidents and troubleshooting
- Document infrastructure and runbooks for the team

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, reliable deployments
- Minimize deployment lead time and manual effort
- Maintain high system availability and performance
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) for incidents

### Typical Communication
- CI/CD pipeline configurations and runbooks
- Infrastructure documentation and architecture diagrams
- Deployment release notes and checklist
- Incident post-mortems and lessons learned
- Collaboration on performance and security requirements

### Key Interactions
- Works with Developers on code quality standards and deployment readiness
- Collaborates with QA/Testing to automate test execution and environment setup
- Partners with Project Managers on release scheduling and risk mitigation
- Supports incident response coordination with Support/Customer Success

---

## QA/Testing

### Role Summary
QA and Testing professionals ensure that features meet acceptance criteria, quality standards, and user expectations. They design test strategies, automate tests, and identify issues before they reach production.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain test plans and test cases
- Perform manual testing for acceptance criteria validation
- Develop and maintain automated test suites
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Collaborate on Definition of Done and quality standards
- Perform smoke and regression testing before releases

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, defect-free features
- Reduce production incidents and support tickets
- Enable faster feedback loops through automation
- Maintain test coverage and traceability

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Defect reports and test result summaries
- Acceptance criteria review and sign-off
- Quality metrics and coverage dashboards
- Collaboration during sprint planning and retrospectives

### Key Interactions
- Works with Developers on test automation and acceptance criteria clarification
- Collaborates with UX/UI Designers on usability testing
- Partners with Product Managers on acceptance criteria validation
- Coordinates with DevOps Engineers on test environment provisioning

---

## Support / Customer Success

### Role Summary
Support and Customer Success teams represent the voice of the customer within the organization. They gather user feedback, resolve customer issues, and provide critical insights that inform product improvements and process refinements.

### Responsibilities
- Triage and resolve customer support tickets
- Gather and document customer feedback and feature requests
- Identify recurring issues and escalate systemic problems
- Participate in incident response and customer communication
- Contribute to user documentation and help content
- Share customer insights in retrospectives and planning sessions

### Goals
- Deliver exceptional customer experiences and support
- Reduce support ticket volume through product improvements
- Identify and escalate high-impact customer issues
- Drive customer-informed product decisions
- Enable faster resolution through clear communication

### Typical Communication
- Support tickets and customer feedback summaries
- Incident communications and status updates
- Customer case studies and feedback trends
- Participation in retrospectives with actionable insights
- Feature request prioritization input

### Key Interactions
- Partners with Product Managers to prioritize features based on customer demand
- Provides feedback to Developers on usability issues and user pain points
- Collaborates with UX/UI Designers on usability improvements
- Works with Project Managers on incident communication and escalation
- Supports DevOps/QA in understanding production issues from a customer perspective
